# Grade 2

## The Big Idea

The Divine Blueprint says reality might work like a pattern with rules.

The pattern may:

- repeat at different sizes
- stay inside limits
- check itself
- make a new version of itself

That new version is similar, but not exactly the same.

## A Good Analogy

Think about a recipe.

A recipe tells you how to make cookies.

If you use the same recipe, the cookies are usually similar. But they are not
perfectly identical. One may be a little bigger. One may have more chocolate.

The Divine Blueprint asks whether reality might have a deep recipe like that.

## The Five Main Ideas

1. Patterns can repeat when you zoom in or out.
2. Information has limits.
3. Forces can change at different energy levels.
4. A stable system must agree with itself.
5. Reality might make new versions of its own pattern.

## What The Computer Checks

The computer checks whether the written rules work together.

It checks the math, the tables, and the computer steps.

It also checks the "copy with a twist" idea:

```text
new pattern = similar to old pattern, but not an exact clone
```

## Not Proven Yet

Not proven yet:

Passing tests means the project’s own pieces agree with each other. It does
not prove the whole universe follows these rules.

## Why It Could Be Exciting

If real-world science later agrees with it, this could be a new way to think
about why nature has order, change, and repeating structure.
